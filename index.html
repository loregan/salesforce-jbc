JBC Controls Transformation Canvas

Consolidating EWorks & Salesforce into a Single Enterprise Platform

CONFIDENTIAL - INTERNAL

1. Executive Summary

JBC Controls operates a sophisticated business model handling multi-month projects, scheduled maintenance (PPM), and reactive call-outs. Currently, their operations suffer from a "Digital Handoff Gap." While Sales, Purchasing, and Project Management efficiently utilize a custom-built Salesforce environment, Field Execution and Scheduling rely on EWorks—a siloed system implemented by previous management.

This separation creates manual duplication (e.g., Project Managers emailing Emma to create jobs in EWorks), obscures long-term scheduling visibility, and disconnects field findings from remedial sales opportunities. JBC is seeking a unified platform to consolidate costs and streamline operations.

The Commercial Imperative

Adam Axford (Projects Director) is driving this evaluation. JBC's current Salesforce contract has a renewal deadline at the end of June 2026, requiring formal notice by May 2026. Moving from Professional to Enterprise Edition (required for Field Service) must present a compelling TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) that justifies dropping the EWorks licenses.

3. Key Challenges

The Email Handoff Gap

Currently, a Project Manager hits a button in SF, which sends an email to Emma. Emma must manually re-key that data into EWorks to create an appointment. Contract renewals suffer the same manual disconnect.

Poor Long-Term Scheduling

While Emma uses the weekly Time Planner in EWorks, the Month View is described as "pointless—just a number in a box." JBC loses critical visibility when planning complex commissioning projects months in advance.

Static "Drawn-On" Forms

Technicians fill out RAMS and Health Checks via EWorks, but the forms are literally "PDFs with boxes drawn over them." This static data traps valuable findings—meaning faulty sensors don't automatically trigger remedial sales quotes.

4. Proposed Solution Overview

We propose upgrading JBC’s 25 users to Salesforce Enterprise Edition and deploying Salesforce Field Service (SFS) to act as the unified execution engine.

By leveraging Vanguard Cloud Consulting for implementation, JBC can seamlessly transition away from EWorks before the summer renewal.

  • Unified Architecture: Work Orders are generated automatically and layered directly underneath Adam's existing SF Project records.
  • Empowered Dispatch: A robust Calendar/Gantt allows Emma to manually assign the right tech based on skills (Tier 1/2/3) and "Preferred Resource" history.
  • Data-Driven Forms: Dynamic RAMS and Health Checks executed offline via the SFS Mobile App.

5. Solution Deep Dive

Field Service Console (For Emma)

  • Calendar-Based Diary View: Replacing the EWorks Time Planner with a dynamic Gantt chart. Emma can see 15 diaries at once, spot multi-week gaps, and view the long-term horizon missing from EWorks.
  • Preserving the "Human Element": JBC jobs are bespoke (1-2 a day). Instead of fully automated routing, Emma retains drag-and-drop control, assisted by logic that flags Tier 1/2/3 engineer skills and site familiarity.

Mobile Execution & Compliance

  • Dynamic RAMS (ISO Requirement): Before starting a job, techs must complete digital Risk Assessments via the SFS App, capturing customer signatures on the glass.
  • Knowledge Base Access: Moving data sheets and "toolbox talks" out of Teams directly onto the Work Order for instant field access.

Enterprise Automation & Reporting

Automated Remedial Quoting

Replacing static PDFs means we can use logic. If a tech submits a Health Check flagging a "Faulty Sensor", Flow automatically alerts the Service Sales team to issue a remedial quote.

Keith's Daily Status Dashboard

A live, 15-minute refresh dashboard giving Keith complete operational visibility: technicians arrived safely on-site, tickets opened, and jobs closed.

To help Adam Axford and the JBC team evaluate the TCO of consolidating onto Salesforce, we have provided an overview of the Field Service licensing options. Upgrading to Enterprise Edition is a prerequisite, after which the appropriate Field Service licenses can be applied based on user roles.

For the 13 field engineers and external techs. Includes offline capabilities, dynamic RAMS, digital Health Checks, and step-by-step mobile flows.

Dispatcher License

Agent License

For customer service and intake agents to create work orders, book appointments, and handle initial project handoffs before dispatch.
